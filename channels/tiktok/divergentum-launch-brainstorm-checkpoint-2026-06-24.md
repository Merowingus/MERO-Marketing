# Divergentum TikTok launch brainstorm checkpoint

**Status:** In progress. Resume tomorrow without repeating completed questions.

## Goal

Create a new English-language TikTok channel for Divergentum that drives website visits and
registrations. The first-month primary metric is traffic and `sign_up`, not followers or raw views.

## Decisions already made

- Channel model: **hybrid creator-led channel**.
- Mykola appears on camera regularly, but gameplay remains the main visual proof.
- Content may include:
  - Mykola speaking to camera;
  - vertical gameplay recordings;
  - short stories about game-design and development decisions.
- Spoken language: English from a prepared script.
- Cadence: five videos per week.
- Available production material: face-to-camera, gameplay, mechanics, and development stories.
- Available tools: phone, OBS, and CapCut.
- Initial time budget: flexible, but design the mature routine to fit roughly 5–7 hours per week.
- Approved weekly mix:
  - 2 gameplay wow moments;
  - 1 class or mechanic clip;
  - 1 short story about a design decision;
  - 1 face-to-camera question or observation about solo D&D.
- Typical duration: 12–25 seconds.
- Structure: conflict or curiosity in the first 1–2 seconds, one payoff, short CTA.

## Approved design section 1: account and positioning

Recommended account type: TikTok Business Account, because the campaign needs an immediate website
link. Verify availability during setup because TikTok capabilities can vary by region and account.

- Username: `@divergentum`
- Display name: `Divergentum | Solo D&D`
- Avatar: recognizable Divergentum mark or Dungeon Master portrait
- Bio:

  `Solo D&D adventures with a responsive AI Dungeon Master.`

  `Built and played by one very picky RPG fan.`

  `Start your adventure ↓`

- Profile URL:

  `https://www.divergentum.com/?utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=profile`

- Channel identity: “A creator building the solo D&D game he personally loves to play.”
- Tone: personal and game-focused, not corporate and not primarily a programming account.
- Music rule: use TikTok's Commercial Music Library or audio owned/licensed for commercial use.
